CASE.01 · Client2020 – 2024 · Mumbai

Company A — Case Study

Marketing & Business Development · B2B Technology Reseller · Microsoft Partner

During the pandemic, a mid-sized customer came to us wanting to buy Zoom licences and Google Drive cloud storage. He was the CEO, managing IT himself. Our technical team could see he didn't need any of it, but the conversation kept stalling because the jargon wasn't landing — so they assigned him to me.

I sat with him and explained, in plain language, that his existing Microsoft Enterprise licence already included pretty much everything he was about to pay twice for. We ended up helping him set up Teams and OneDrive across his team, at no additional software cost.

The takeaway: technical jargon can be a wall. Meeting someone where they are isn't a soft skill sitting next to the real work — a lot of the time it is the real work. The deal we didn't close that day turned into a customer who trusted us on bigger things later.

ACAD.01 · Thesis2026 · Solo

Thesis — AI marketing in
privacy-conscious B2B

MA Strategic Marketing Management · ISM Berlin · 2026

A qualitative study of how German B2B firms actually evaluate AI-powered marketing tools in a market where data privacy and institutional trust change every adoption decision. Twelve expert interviews across marketing, sales, IT, and procurement.

The core finding: adoption isn't a yes/no decision, it's a five-layer filter — infrastructure fit, relational appropriateness, governance alignment, regulatory conformity, and reputational credibility. I built this into a framework called the Negotiated Institutional Adoption Framework (NIAF).

Vendors selling into Germany should lead with compliance, prioritise integration, and partner with incumbents rather than trying to disrupt them.
